Registration of Death Certificate

Registration of Certificate of Death at the Local Civil Registrar

Processing

1 hr 35-38 mins

Fee

₱50-150

Who Can Apply

Informant/Family

Deadline

Within 30 Days

Important Registration Information

The registration of Death Certificate (DC) at the MCRO within thirty (30) days is mandatory. Delayed registration of death must be filed following the lapse of the prescribed period of 30 calendar days from the death of a person. Registration of death certificate is free of charge, pursuant to Section 6 of P.D. No. 651.

Requirements

Documents needed for death certificate registration

For Death in Hospital/Clinic

  • Certificate of Death duly signed by attending physician
  • Valid ID of informant
  • Embalmer's Certification

For Death at Home (Unattended)

  • Information about the deceased (full name, date of birth, date of death, etc.)
  • Valid ID of informant
  • Embalmer's Certification
  • MHO Certification of Cause of Death

For Delayed Registration (After 30 Days)

  • Affidavit for Delayed Registration of Death
  • PSA negative certification
  • Supporting documents (hospital records, burial records, etc.)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a fee for death certificate registration?
Registration of death certificate is free of charge, pursuant to Section 6 of P.D. No. 651. However, you need to pay for the Burial Permit (₱50.00) or Transfer Permit (₱100.00).
What is the deadline for registering a death certificate?
The death certificate must be registered within 30 calendar days from the date of death. After this period, it will be considered delayed registration and will require additional documents.
What is the difference between Burial Permit and Transfer Permit?
A Burial Permit (₱50.00) is required if the deceased will be buried in any cemetery in Solano. A Transfer Permit (₱100.00) is required if the remains will be transferred to another municipality for burial.
Who can be the informant for death registration?
The informant is usually a family member or relative of the deceased who has knowledge of the facts of death. For hospital deaths, the attending physician provides the medical information.
How long does the registration process take?
For death in hospital/clinic: approximately 1 hour and 38 minutes. For death at home: approximately 1 hour and 35 minutes. This includes time for embalmer certification and MHO review.
